Understanding REACH

REACH is a regulation of the European Union. It stands for Registration, Evaluation, Authorization and Restriction of Chemicals. It entered into force on June 1, 2007. It was adopted to imporve the protection of human health and the environment from the risks that can be posed by chemicals, while enhancing the competitiveness of the EU chemicals industry. It also promotes alternative methods for hazard assessment of substances in order to reduce the number of tests on animals.
REACH applies to chemical substances, not only those used in industries, but also those that are used on daily basis. Therefore, it has an impact on most companies in EU.
Companies have to demonstrate to ECHA how a substance can be safely used. If the risks cannot be managed, authorities can restrict the use of substances in different ways.

Process

REACH establishes procedures for collecting and assessing information on the properties and hazards of substances.
Step 1: Companies need to register their substances.
Step 2: ECHA receives and evaluates registrations for their compliance, and the EU Member States evaluate selected substances to clarify inintial concerns for human health or for the environment. Authorities assess whether the risks of substances can be managed.
Step 3: If the risks are unmanageable, the substance is banned. Otherwise authorised.


Effect of REACH on companies

Manufacturers: Companies that produce chemicals have important responsibilities under REACH.
Importers: Companies that import chemicals from outside EU/EEA have important responsibilities under REACH.
Donwstream Users: Companies that handle chemicals have some responsibility under REACH.
Companies established outside the EU: Such companies are not bound by EU. Rather, importers within EU that import chemicals from companies outside EU have important responsibilities under REACH.
